## How to host a meeting

<mark>2025-05-28</mark>

### Greeting

Hi, everyone. Ray, will Mike join the meeting?

*(I'm afraid not. He's off today)*

All right. Could you take the note for me?

*(Sure)*

### Introduction

Yesterday, we received feedback from our client.

Let me describe the issue first. As you can see, after the user clicks the deposit button, a popup to the 3rd-party vendor is shown. Then the user cancels this transaction by closing this popup and goes back to our side. The user is expected to see something like "Transaction Canceled" on our site. However, it is actually pending all the time.

We were not able to reproduce this issue in our stage environment, and that's why we're talking about it here.

OK. Now I want to hear your opinions. Feel free to ask if there's anything unclear to you.

### BrainStorm

*(Did this issue affect all vendors, or only one vendor?)*

### Discuss

Obviously, we need more information about it. We don't even know how many vendors are affected. Additionally, are there any other resources we should ask our client for?

*(We can ask them for the accurate time when they noticed the problem.)*

### Conclusion

I'll talk with our client and ask them for these information.

### Future Work

We're going to have another meeting to discuss how to resolve this problem and provide our client with the estimated time on next Monday.

## Leadership & Team Management Sentences

<mark>2026-04-30</mark>

### Leadership Character

*What a leader should be — integrity, capability, and presence.*

1. What I want to emphasize is that being approachable is not just about saying nice things. Beyond that, a leader must also have sufficient capability.
2. A person who is not sincere and only cares about his own interests. Your team members will not respect you.
3. If a leader cannot help you with specific problems, how can he be a leader?
4. He needs to have sufficient experience, both technical and commercial.
5. Whenever we feel work-related pressure, he is able to show up and help us.

### Innovation & Progress

*Pushing the team forward and embracing change.*

1. If a leader only sticks to the old ways of working, the team will never make progress.
2. It would be great if you can retain existing knowledge while continuously learning new knowledge.
3. Be able to keep the team moving forward.
4. He is able to push everyone out of their comfort zones to try new things.

### Talent & Delegation

*Recognizing strengths, assigning work, and enabling potential.*

1. Having someone on the team who is more familiar with the business than you is better than having someone who knows nothing at all.
2. What would you do if there is someone on the team who is more capable than you?
3. Be able to assign work properly and review it.
4. How can you give them instructions and hand-hold them while still enabling them to reach their full potential?
5. He knows everyone's strengths well and puts them in the right positions.

### Team Dynamics

*Atmosphere, communication, and handling tough situations.*

1. This helps create a more democratic and energetic atmosphere for the entire team.
2. A work group of four people can have six chat groups.
3. After you've tried all the positive measures, if none of them work, what will you do?
4. Since we need to communicate with him every day.

## English Lesson Notes: Team Management & Leadership

<mark>2026-04-09</mark>

### 1. Organization & Goals

*Focuses on corporate output and structural alignment.*

- **Organizational performance**: 组织绩效
- **Functional department**: 职能部门
- **Drive team success**: 驱动团队成功
- **Creative innovation**: 创意创新

### 2. Leadership Responsibilities

*Behaviors and attitudes expected from effective managers.*

- **Bring out the potential**: 激发潜力
- **Be accountable for**: 对……负责
- **Keep track of**: 跟踪/监控（进度、数据等）
- **Down-to-earth**: 务实的 / 接地气的
- **Go back on decision**: 改变主意 / 食言

### 3. Teamwork & Atmosphere

*The core of team cohesion and mutual interaction.*

- **Promote team spirit**: 弘扬团队精神
- **Nurture team dynamic**: 培养团队动态
- **Mutual trust**: 互信
- **Closely knit**: 组织严密的 / 关系紧密的
- **Liven up**: 活跃（气氛）
- **Commitment**: 承诺 / 投入 / 奉献
- **Interpersonal relationship**: 人际关系
- **Pay back**: 回报 / 报答

### 4. Individual Growth & Traits

*How individuals function and develop within a group setting.*

- **Individuality**: 个性 / 个人特质
- **Boost confidence**: 增强信心
- **Develop social skills**: 发展社交技巧
- **Overcritical**: 吹毛求疵的 / 过分挑剔的
